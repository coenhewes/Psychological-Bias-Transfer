# Methodology

## The research question
Does fine-tuning a base LLM with Population-Based Training (PBT) on a corpus rich in
cognitive-distortion language cause the model to *express* those distortion patterns
more readily in open-ended first-person generation — and does this hold specifically
for the treatment corpus vs a matched control, and across model architectures?

## Corpus design
- **Treatment corpus** (`data/processed/synthetic_treatment.jsonl`): first-person posts
  exhibiting one of 4 markers — rumination, catastrophizing, doom_framing,
  certainty_collapse. Generated by Gemini from everyday stressors, operationalized by
  exemplars (see `evaluation/marker_definitions.py`).
- **Control corpus** (`data/processed/synthetic_control.jsonl`): matched on topic/stressor
  distribution but WITHOUT the distortion pattern — same situations, neutral expression.
  Built via subreddit matching (anxiety/ptsd/domesticviolence → relationships/assistance)
  per `data/processed/build_manifest.json`.
- Both ~15.2k tokens. The control isolates "talking about stressors" from "expressing
  the distortion."

## Training (PBT / QLoRA)
- QLoRA (4-bit NF4, r=16, alpha=32) on the base model, max_steps=250, effective batch 32,
  fixed shuffle seed so ONLY the init seed varies across runs (isolable seed effect).
- Treatment arm trains on treatment corpus; control arm on control corpus.
- Hyperparameters fixed in `config/training_config.yaml` (constant across all runs).

## Evaluation protocol (THE CRITICAL FIX)
Base models are NOT instruction-tuned. The original eval wrapped prompts in a
chat/assistant template and called `model.generate` — on a base model this yields
gibberish/refusals, not continuation, so marker rates were ~0.1% in BOTH arms
(treatment slightly LOWER) → a false null.

**Corrected protocol:** first-person continuation. The model is given an incomplete
first-person opener and continues it (`generate_outputs.py --first-person`). This is the
natural mode of a base model and elicits genuine rumination-style text. Validated:
treatment-seed17 produced 8.0% lexicon-marked continuations (vs ~0.1% broken), with real
first-person distress ("99% of the time when they say that, it's a layoff...").

## Markers & judging
4 markers, each operationalized by 5-8 exemplars (not prose definitions) to avoid
definition variance between judges:
- rumination, catastrophizing, doom_framing, certainty_collapse.

Two independent LLM judges (per design-doc mandate of ≥2 judges for reliability):
1. **Gemini `gemini-3.5-flash`** (`evaluation/judge.py`, local, 20 threads) — primary.
2. **gpt-oss-20b** (OpenAI OSS, 4-bit, Vertex L4, `evaluation/judge_gptoss.py`) —
   independent lab/training, used for inter-judge agreement (kappa). A second Gemini
   variant was rejected as same-family (correlated bias).

Why two judges: a single judge (especially Gemini scoring its own lab's model family)
is a known circularity risk. Agreement between Gemini and gpt-oss is a real reliability
signal.

## Statistics
- **Primary test:** paired t-test, treatment vs control, within each model, across seeds
  (marker frequency per 1,000 generated tokens).
- **Factorial:** corpus × model ANOVA — valid only with ≥2 models (the reason Qwen is
  included; with Llama alone it is singular and skipped).
- **Reliability:** Cohen's kappa between the two judges on each marker.
- **Power:** post-hoc, given observed effect sizes — borderline markers (doom_framing,
  certainty_collapse) needed ~2 seeds for 80% power; 5 seeds locks them.

## Known limitations (to address before write-up)

  result does not speak to instruction-following behavior.
- Control arm near-zero on all markers; a base-model-reference checkpoint would confirm
  the control isn't an artifact of broken generation (unlikely post-fix, but worth a check).
